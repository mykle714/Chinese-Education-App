### The two sentences the reviewer rejected — 一意
I rejected two of my own generated sentences (`一意坚持`, `一意要`) as `unnatural_phrasing`.
**Bare 一意 is not used as a free adverb in modern Mandarin** — it survives essentially only
inside the chengyu 一意孤行; a native speaker says 执意 or 一心一意. The repairs use the chengyu:

> 他一意孤行，谁的话都不听。 — He went his own way stubbornly, and he would not listen to anyone.
> 他一意孤行地做了决定，现在很后悔。 — He made the decision stubbornly on his own, and now he regrets it.

> ⚠ **Recommendation: 一意 probably should not be a standalone discoverable card.** Its
> definitions are fine, but every natural sentence for it embeds the chengyu, which also risks
> the segmenter tokenizing 一意孤行 as one unit so the target word never highlights. Worth a
> human decision.

## 8. Findings

### ⚠ Finding 1 — planner over-reports on legacy rows (carried over)
`pendingSteps` judges from the `enrichmentLog` stamp; scripts judge from the column. Pre-stamping
rows show populated columns as pending. Confirmed harmless (deterministic scripts reported
`Found 0`), but it inflates plan counts.

### ⚠ Finding 2 — "unchanged" rows are never stamped (new, more consequential)
When a reorder produces a result identical to what is stored, the script reports `Unchanged` and
writes **no stamp**. Those rows (一些, 一直, 三 in round 1; 2 more in round 2) stay permanently
at `defs_v = 0` and will be re-selected, re-prompted, and re-answered on every future run —
wasted work forever. **Fix: stamp on `Unchanged` too**, since the script version genuinely did
run and did produce a verdict.

### ⚠ Finding 3 — export accounting misses hand-rolled loops (carried over)
The three multi-call scripts report a healthy prompt capture as `FAILED / Errors: N` because they
don't use the shared runner's `err.oracleExport` branch. Purely cosmetic but actively misleading:
it caused me to mis-diagnose "Failed 2" as validator rejections when they were just the next link
in the chain.

### ⚠ Finding 4 — targetPos accuracy vs. POS coverage conflict
For 一起's measure-word sense I tagged `classifier` (accurate). The pipeline then demanded a
separate sentence covering the declared `noun` POS and would not converge until I re-tagged the
same sense as `noun`. Accuracy and coverage are in tension; the declared POS list wins. Worth
deciding which is authoritative.
